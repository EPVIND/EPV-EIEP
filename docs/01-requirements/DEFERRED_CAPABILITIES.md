# Deferred Capabilities

Deferred means intentionally excluded from MVP acceptance, not rejected from the enterprise vision.

## Business and commercial

- CRM, opportunity management, advanced estimating, proposal generation, contract lifecycle, billing, cash flow, payroll, and full ERP functions.
- Advanced job costing, earned value, resource leveling, and claims/change analytics.

## Advanced execution

- Full welding, NDE, PWHT, pressure testing, coatings, insulation, fireproofing, electrical/instrumentation, loop checks, commissioning, and maintenance suites beyond what the pilot needs to reference.
- Full structural CNC/DSTV, Tekla/SDS2/Advance Steel/IFC, robotic welding, and machine integration.
- Full civil estimating, survey/point-cloud, machine control, and geospatial information system.

## Instrument and external integration

- Direct PMI analyzer docking/import, NDE equipment ingestion, laboratory interfaces, laser scanners, and industrial equipment telemetry.
- Deep Bluebeam, P6, Microsoft Project, accounting, payroll, ERP, vendor, owner, and regulator integrations beyond approved MVP exchange needs.

## Enterprise and product expansion

- Fleet and enterprise asset management.
- Advanced client analytics, AI assistance, predictive quality, automated estimating, and optimization.
- Owner digital twin and long-term operational maintenance history.
- Commercial multi-tenancy, marketplace, licensing, and customer self-service administration.

## Deferral controls

- Do not implement a deferred feature through an unreviewed shortcut.
- When a deferred capability becomes necessary, define requirements, data and security impacts, integrations, acceptance criteria, migration, and product-owner priority.
- Architecture should preserve stable identifiers and extension points without building speculative complexity.
