## Direction

The task owner stated that enterprise-program capability assumptions are based on
the supplied dashboard. The dashboard is therefore the product-vision baseline for
the intended EIEP surface. It is not, by itself, evidence that a capability is
implemented, validated, licensed, connected to a production provider, or authorized
for production use.

The display's example project names, counts, percentages, people, timestamps,
statuses, version, storage usage, and footer year are illustrative presentation
content only. They are not controlled EPV data, acceptance targets, staffing
assignments, licensed code/standard content, or production-readiness evidence.

## Delivery rule

A vision surface may appear in navigation as one of: `available`, `pilot`,
`in implementation`, or `planned`. It may be labeled `available` only when its
requirements, authorization, immutable/audited records, API, usable workflow,
PostgreSQL persistence, negative tests, traceability, and required production adapter
evidence are present. A tile, mock-up, or route stub is never acceptance evidence.

Cross-cutting identities are reused: organizations, people, projects, facilities,
systems/areas/WBS/work packages, files, document revisions, materials/heats,
qualifications, equipment/calibration, deficiencies, completion boundaries, audit,
jobs, and exports. No module creates an incompatible duplicate identity simply to
match the dashboard presentation.
