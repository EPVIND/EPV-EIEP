# Product Vision

## Vision statement

EIEP will be EPV's controlled operating platform for full-scope industrial fabrication and construction. It will connect planning, documents, materials, work execution, quality, subcontractors, commissioning, and turnover through one auditable digital thread.

## Customers and users

The platform serves EPV leadership, project teams, engineering, procurement, document control, shop and field supervision, craft personnel, quality, safety, subcontractors, vendors, third-party inspectors and laboratories, Authorized Inspectors, clients, commissioning teams, and system administrators. Each sees only the information and actions authorized for their role and assigned scope.

## Problems to solve

- Fragmented spreadsheets, forms, email, shared drives, and turnover binders.
- Loss of material identity after receiving, cutting, fabrication, or issue.
- Weak linkage between current drawings, work packages, inspections, and installed assets.
- Late discovery of missing quality records and turnover deliverables.
- Inconsistent control when work is subcontracted or delegated to a lower tier.
- Duplicate records for the same spool, member, foundation, weld, or equipment item.
- Manual progress claims that are disconnected from accepted installed quantities.
- Production/demo contamination and uncertain record authority.

## Desired outcomes

- A current, permission-controlled view of project and work-package status.
- Traceable materials, work, inspections, deficiencies, approvals, and turnover.
- Repeatable release gates based on project requirements and qualified approvals.
- Early visibility of constraints and missing records.
- Equivalent control for self-perform, subcontracted, and blended work.
- System-based turnover that owners and operations teams can navigate.
- A modular architecture that can grow without duplicating core objects.

## Product principles

1. One controlled object, many authorized views.
2. Configuration over hard-coded project or customer behavior.
3. Evidence over unexplained scores or status claims.
4. Continuous turnover, not end-of-project document assembly.
5. Modular capability on a shared foundation.
6. Human accountability at engineering, quality, safety, and release decisions.
7. Offline-tolerant field workflows where connectivity is unreliable.
8. Security, auditability, and environment isolation by design.

## Success measures for the first production release

- A pilot project completes the approved vertical slice without a parallel uncontrolled log.
- Every controlled revision, material decision, inspection outcome, NCR disposition, and turnover inclusion is attributable and auditable.
- A user cannot access an unassigned project or perform an unauthorized release.
- Training data cannot enter production builds or production databases.
- Required turnover records can be identified before system completion.
- Field users can complete designated workflows on supported tablets.
