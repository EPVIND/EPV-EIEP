# Workflow: Civil and Concrete

## Civil sequence

Survey/control -> clearing/demolition -> excavation/dewatering -> subgrade/proof/soil acceptance -> fill/bedding/lifts -> compaction/testing -> underground placement/testing -> backfill release -> grading/as-built.

Each feature links to current drawing, location/coordinates, WBS/work package, material/source, inspection/test, quantities, performer, and dependent release.

## Foundation and concrete sequence

1. Establish foundation identity, drawing revision, survey control, design attributes, and equipment/structure relationship.
2. Accept excavation/subgrade and soil/compaction prerequisites.
3. Inspect formwork, reinforcement, couplers, cover, embeds, sleeves, waterstops, grounding, anchor bolts, cleanliness, and access.
4. Release pre-pour hold points by authorized parties.
5. Record placement ID, mix design, batch tickets, supplier, quantity, times, weather, temperatures, slump/air/unit weight as required, placement/consolidation/finish/curing, samples, crew/equipment, and photos.
6. Link laboratory samples/results and strength acceptance.
7. Complete repairs, dimensional/as-built survey, grout, baseplate/equipment release, and turnover.

## Gates

- Current drawing and approved mix/materials.
- Required pre-pour inspections and embeds verified.
- Test technician/laboratory and equipment status valid.
- Dependent erection/equipment setting blocked until configured strength, dimension, and foundation acceptance are satisfied.

## Subcontractors

Concrete supplier, testing laboratory, rebar installer, pump, surveyor, and lower tiers remain individually identified and approved as required; one contractor's submission does not equal EPV acceptance.
