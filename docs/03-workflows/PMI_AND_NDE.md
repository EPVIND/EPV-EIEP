# Workflow: PMI and NDE

## PMI planning

1. Determine applicable component/material population and governing project rule.
2. Select 100 percent, approved sampling percentage, risk-based, weld/filler, final verification, or client-specific plan.
3. Confirm method capability: XRF, OES when carbon/light elements require it, or other approved method.
4. Assign qualified inspector and approved instrument with valid calibration/daily verification/reference standard evidence.

## PMI execution

1. Positively identify/scan the material, component, weld, spool, member, or location.
2. Confirm required grade/specification and exact governing record/drawing revision.
3. Record method, instrument, verification, readings/elements/evidence, identified alloy, required versus actual, location, timestamp, and inspector.
4. Determine pass/fail under the approved procedure; do not let the application invent metallurgy acceptance.
5. On pass, route configured review and material/work release.
6. On fail or invalid test, quarantine affected scope, preserve evidence, create/link NCR, define extent-of-condition review, and require reinspection after disposition.

## NDE handoff

NDE requests identify exact weld/component, method, extent, procedure/technique, acceptance reference, stage, due date, and assigned organization/personnel. Results retain examination date, personnel qualification, equipment/calibration, report, indications, result, repairs, re-examination, and final acceptance.

## Controls

- Raw instrument/report files are preserved with hashes and parsing/import metadata.
- Import does not bypass inspector review or acceptance authority.
- Expired qualifications, instrument validity, wrong method capability, unverified identity, or superseded acceptance basis blocks acceptance.
- PMI and NDE remain distinct inspection types even when shared equipment, personnel, or turnover groupings apply.
