# Workflow: Subcontractor Control

## Master qualification

1. Create organization and subcontractor profile.
2. Record legal/tax metadata, approved trades/scopes, licenses, insurance, bonding, safety, quality/certifications, personnel/equipment capability, geography, labor model, client qualification, and performance history.
3. Verify evidence, issuer, effective/expiration dates, exceptions, and approval scope.

## Project sourcing and award

1. Define WBS/work-package scope, responsibilities, inclusions/exclusions, deliverables, inspections, schedule, commercial controls, and lower-tier rules.
2. Verify bidder qualification and project/client prerequisites.
3. Issue controlled bid documents and clarifications.
4. Record evaluation and award authorization; link subcontract/PO and schedule of values as applicable.

## Mobilization

Evaluate executed authorization, insurance/licenses, safety plan/JHAs/training, quality plan/ITP/procedures, competent/qualified persons, equipment inspections, lower-tier approvals, emergency contacts, client access, and required submittals. Missing required items block mobilization release.

## Execution

- Assign current documents, work packages, activities, quantities, inspections, hold points, and turnover requirements.
- Capture manpower/equipment/daily production, constraints, submissions, quality/safety records, changes, NCR/punch actions, and forecast.
- EPV verifies progress and acceptance; claimed or invoiced completion does not establish installed/accepted progress.

## Payment and closeout

Support validation of schedule of values, verified quantities/progress, accepted quality records, approved changes, insurance, payroll/lien documentation where applicable, retainage, backcharges, punch, and turnover. Payment decisions remain in the approved commercial system/workflow.

## Access

Portal users see only their organization and assigned scope. They never see EPV margin, other bidder/subcontractor pricing, unrelated customer data, or internal confidential evaluations. EPV retains final control and acceptance.
